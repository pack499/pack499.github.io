About Pinewood Derby

Since it was first introduced in 1953 by Don Murphy, a Cubmaster in California, Pine Wood Derby(PWD) has become one of the most popular and favorite activity for cubscouts. It is one of the most celebrated tradition in Cubscouts. Pack499 had a blasting performance in 2022 with multiple awards in various categories. PWD is about creativity, innovation and parent/caregiver - scout collaboration span multiple weeks to build a car from a single piece of derby and compete among the cubscouts.

What to expect in this workshop ?

  • Volunteers will help you to cut the pinewood in straightline cuts as per the markings done on the wood block. There could be sanding tools that you may use with the help of volunteers. Scout will have to work at their own pace at privacy of their own place to further shape and assemble the car. As there are tens of people need help from both the packs, each participant may get around 5 to 10 mins of volunteer time and so come prepared with design.
  • Scout participating in the race must be present at the workshop to get volunteer’s help.
  • Check with the PWD chair for the appointment booking system.
  • If you have the tools for the workshop and want to contribute, you are most welcome!

Car inspection important info:

  • Use only the provided block and the wheels to build the car.
  • Do not lubricate the car wheels yet.
  • All 4 wheens must touch the surface.
  • Height, length, width and Weight should be within the set limits.
  • Use provided slots for the axles.

Please note, the inspection and race date and times have no room for adjustments. Only the cars inspected and collected on Car-Inspection day will be eligible to participate in the race. So please follow the communication and schedules closely.
